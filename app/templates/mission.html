MISSION/VISION

 

To promote a culture of peace and restoration of justice through Advocacy, economic empowerment and community development.

The organization shall have the following principal objectives:

1)    To train communities in peace initiative programs such as Alternative to Violence, mediation, trauma counseling, and active non-violence.

2)    To help the communities start up economic empowerment/development projects.

3)    Train communities, especially youth, to be self-reliant.

4)    To have advocacy programs working to promote justice and civic education.

5)    To sensitize communities on gender equity and promotion of equity in economic development.

6)    To raise, mobilize and disburse funds and other resources for the promotion of women and youth through the community structures.

7)    To access resources by personal or written appeal and public meetings in order to procuring contributions (both financial and volunteer hours) that will strengthen sustainability and the capacity to maintain and grow TCSC programs.

 

OUR TEAM

 

Getry Agizah

Getry Agizah was born in Western Kenya as the 7th born in a family of nine children. Getrys father died when Getry was young leaving her mother caring for many children on her own. As the third wife to her father, Getrys mother was left with nothing. The struggles that they faced had a great impact on Getry, initially shaping a growing anger and ideas of revenge. However, Getry found a new path following participation in an Alternatives to Violence Project (AVP) workshop. Her life was transformed by this new understanding of the cycles of violence and the impact of trauma. She began working with Quaker organizations promoting peace, and has become a leader and skilled trainer of many community healing approaches.

 

Getry is a co-founder of TCSC and has worked to promote peace and healing across Kenya. She is passionate in her commitment to this work, and has been a courageous and dedicated support to community leaders on Mount Elgon. She works closely with communities struggling to overcome the impact of violence and trauma, as well as ex-militia seeking reconciliation and reintegration into their communities.

 

Getry lives in Kakamega, Kenya with her husband Joseph Thuita and 3 children. Her youngest daughter was adopted due to the loss of her mother in childbirth. Getrys house is lively with the presence of many who seek safety in hard times, as well as friendship and mutual support.

 

Erastus Chesondi

Erastus Chesondi lives in Chebyuk, on Mount Elgon with his wife Violet and 7 adopted children. For many years, he has provided leadership as a highly respected pastor on Mount Elgon. However, this leadership role was amplified following the 2008 post-election violence. Having personally survived a brutal attack Erastus participated in a HROC training that changed his life. He joined efforts to build TCSC and the Peace Center and takes the lead on all Peace Center community projects. Erastus leads the Childrens Ministry, and mobilizes extraordinary participation and commitment from across the mountain.
