The Mt Elgon region suffered at the hands of the SLDF militia group. The Sabot Land Defence Force.

Established to rid Mt. Elgon of resettled people from another group who they felt were taking their rightful lands.

The scares of pain, loss, grief and trauma of the many years land war fade away after UPCD/TCSC has and is still doing tremendous work through training for peace and trauma healing that is extensive in this area.

The youths and opinion leaders have been used in the past to rock violence in the community due to their vulnerability hence being forced to join and form militia groups.

Most of them lost their lives while others succumbed to serious injuries.

Healing is a process and dealing with painful emotions and trauma.

Through Healing and Rebuilding Our Community (HROC) we have done a big number of three-day trainings having 20 participants in each workshop.

Objective:

1)      To train communities in peace initiative programs such as Alternative to Violence, Mediation, Trauma Counseling, and Active Non-Violence.

2)      To support and protect Innocent Girls and rape survivors and any other special Group like Orphans.

3)      Support children who are vulnerable and orphan to be able to access smooth education and stable.

4)      To help the communities start up economic Empowerment projects.

5)      Train communities especially the youths on self-reliance.

6)      To implement advocacy programs towards justice and civic education.

7)      To sensitize the communities on Gender Equity for promotion of equity in development.

8)      To raise, mobilize and disburse funds and other resources for the promotion of women and youth through the community structures.

 

Descriptions of the workshops:

ALTERNATIVES TO VIOLENCE PROGRAM

In AVP workshops, participants become aware of “Transformative Power,” a core concept in AVP.

It has the capacity to alter individuals’ views of themselves as well as their interactions with their families, community, and society.

AVP workshops promote group reconciliation across divisive lines of identity, focusing on building relationships and structures that can resolve current and future conflicts without recourse to violence.

Thus, for example, participants engage in group activities in which they have an opportunity to directly experience more positive ways of resolving inter-personal conflicts.

This reflects the popular-education methodology on which all workshops are built.

These three-day workshops are intensive, experiential, and involve participants as agents in the learning process.

Each lesson aims to teach on three levels: the heart or emotional level, the head or intellectual level, and the hand or practical level.

The workshop content is drawn from the participants’ own lives, acknowledging that participants themselves are the experts about what is needed in their communities.

This builds up on five themes which are AFFIRMATION, COMMUNICATION, COOPERATION, and COMMUNITY BUILT ON TRUST AND CONFLICT RESOLUTION respectively.

 

HEALING AND REBUILDING OUR COMUNITIES

Healing and Rebuilding Our Communities (HROC) workshops were built upon the AVP framework, but were adapted to address trauma healing and reconciliation, originally in the context of Burundi and Rwanda, and have now been adapted to Kenya and the US.

HROC workshops, for example, use as their conceptual framework Judith Herman’s map to trauma recovery (Trauma and Recovery), which culminates with reconnecting participants to their communities.

To achieve this, a careful process is followed which begins, following Herman’s model, with the establishment of safety and trust.

At the start, it is common for participants to be wary of attending workshops fearing they might be a trap where they will be attacked, sent to prison, or killed.

Through experiential activities and cooperative exercises, participants begin to relax. Ground rules are set by the group itself to increase the sense of safety.

The second stage in Herman’s model is “Remembrance and Mourning.” There are two African proverbs that emphasize the importance of speaking out about one’s pain:

“The family that does not talk, dies” and “The man who is sick must tell the whole world.” Broken trust and dismantled families have impeded that intuitive process of healing, but it is widely accepted in the cultures here as an important step in the journey toward healing.

In the workshops a forum is created for participants to pay tribute to their losses and to share their grief with others.

Often, victim and perpetrator are sitting in the same room. All come from the same community.

This process helps to humanize the “other” thereby laying the foundation for the third and final stage, “Reconnection.”

Many program participants report having felt very isolated in their grief and their reactions to the trauma they have experienced.

The workshops become an important first step in realizing that they are not alone.

Another important aspect of reconnection is the process of rebuilding relationships across “ethnic” or “tribal” lines, thereby strengthening the fabric of communities torn apart by a long history of violence.

After the workshops, we train healing companions from each community involved who will help the victims through their journey of healing.

 

HEALTH NUTRITION AND HYGENE

Project that is all round with vulnerable girls and orphans who are exposed to risk of insecurity and early pregnancy.

In recent time cases of defilement has gone rampant. We do counselling to the victims and walk with them in search for justice.

Provide school uniforms for the Orphans and vulnerable, and offer medical support for the sick.

Take the rape victims to safe home of friends who volunteer to protect them.

And also provide on a quarterly basis nutrition for the young people as we talk to the parents on how important it is to raise balanced diet for the families.

We do community clean-up day depending on the community need. Be it jigger clean up or environment clean up.

 

ACHIEVEMENTS

 

Peace Trainings: -

 

Midwives: - the region has total number of 89 traditional midwives, out of that number, only ten have been trained on midwifery.

79 traditional midwives are working without experience thus when faced with complicated pregnancies e.g. bridge babies it leads to high mortality rate.

Many children are born and their mothers die in the process just because the midwife handling her did one or two things wrong.

There were also cases of deep trauma with the women in relation to the kind of experience of death as they help other women have their babies or gender based violence.

We did trainings for trauma healing, HROC and finally some months back we trained at least 42 midwives on modern midwifery.

At the moment, more mothers are able to be guided on importance of prenatal process since they attend at least two out of four antenatal care clinics and at least a good number of these mothers’ turn-up to have their babies in hospital and later continue with postnatal care.

 

Orphans support: - during the terror of Sabaot Land Defence Force (SLDF) in 2006, many people were killed and young girls raped as the clashes were happening.

This left a big number of orphans in the region. So far, we have done tremendous training on Trauma Healing for the victims of SLDF.

Two years ago, we realized no one was paying attention to the pain and lose the children have gone through.

Exactly a year ago, the director Getry Agizah was doing a dialogue and in the neighbourhood, a woman died while giving birth. She took the little girl and we realized the woman had five other kids who on the look were malnutrition and had developed scabies.

She took in the new born and asked Erustus to keep the five siblings after realizing that their father had died five months before.

This provoked the urge to find out what is a life of an Orphan in various community. Young girls get married very early because they need security and safety.

And they end up in wrong marriages.

So far for this one year, we have a total number of 28 Orphans put in friends’ homes and the Organization support with food, medication, and school supply like books, pens and uniforms.

This is temporary as we prepare plans of building a rescue centre and have them accommodated within a premise they can feel safe and comfortable.

 

Defilement cases: - Culture has it in the region that when kids grow to talk and they walk and know to interact, that is when they are moved to stay in a one house leaving the main house for their parents.

This mostly is one house in a neighbourhood where various family have their children stay together. And they stay in the same house despite gender.

Some women have opted into alcohol forgetting their responsibilities of taking care of their homes and their children.

This exposes the girl child at risk. In the month of August this year we have heard 5 cases of fathers defiling their children who are 10 years and below.

One of the case was fresh and severe that we had to take the victim who had a big cut to hospital. So far in the month of September three of these cases have been taken with the seriousness it deserves, the police have done their part and arrested the perpetrators.

The young innocent girls who are age, 9years, 7 years and 11years have been moved to safe homes temporarily as we organize what to do.

We provide food and clothing and medical attention for the kids and having in mind we moved theme to a new school to avoid stereotypes.

 

Civic Leadership: - since the clashes, most of the community members never registered as voters, this meant that whoever was aspiring they fight among themselves and who has power takes it over.

Reason being if one voted their person, and the militia group finds a vote they did not want, all the people around the market will be killed if found.

In 2012, we did lots of civic Education and voter Education that when we went to 2013 election, we have a shift from 18% of registered voter to 56%.

With this Kenya is going into Election Next Year 2017, we hope to keep doing Civic Education and Shift more to our target which is 75%.

 

Health Trainings: - there has been misconceptions on reproductive health information among the young youth in Mt Elgon, a lot of myth supported by cultural process and ignorance in the community. One of the major myth that I heard most young men share is HIV and AIDS can only be transmitted through sexual intercourse, and if one sleep with a young virgin girl they get cured of the disease. The last three months we have moved to six schools in the area namely Chelebei Secondary, Kikai Friends Secondary, St Johns Secondary, St Teresa Secondary, Chepkurkur Primary and Kimabole Secondary.

We selected this school because they have been put on the media on early pregnancy cases.

In this school we visited, young men and women have no Idea About Sexually Transmitted Infection (STI) and mode of transmission.

As we supplied sanitary towels and pants to the girls, and underwear to the boys, we have heard 18 young people asking for referral to hospital using the government program of youth Friendly.

Some have been having severe STI but were shy to go to the hospital.

We are working on collaborating with the largest mobile network providers to do a medical camp and have all villages come out and check on their health, status and how they can live. Again, check on the children and talk about nutrition, this is because parents prefer to sell food they harvest which is nutritious and buy other things that they admire and families suffer from malnutrition.

 

Community Development Land: - this is the one greatest achievement that we can’t ignore. As we did trainings way back in 2007 to 2010, most people cried and asked how it can happen we have a place that can be a peace centre. If people meet challenges they go there for consultation and refresher. Yes, we again threw the challenge to the community members asking since this is what they need, we can support them get it. Not by buying the land, rather make cards for them to sale and as people buy and give money, so do we get closer to the having of a peace centre.

And thus in 2013, we raised money among friends of Mt Elgon and the community and the two and half acre’s parcel was found, which the peace centre Mt Elgon is.

We are currently using the farm to plant Irish potatoes and Cabbage to support the children who are needy in the area.

 

STATEMENT OF NEED

United for Peace and Community Development, has done tremendous work with community of Mt Elgon over the past 10 years since 2009.

This kind of healing work was started on the mountain shortly after election violence took grip in an already violent community.

After several trainings of the Ex-Militia Members, it has given us a new page, a new leaf and it has been like a new dawn in the peace work.

After these past 10 years, we are now realizing that the needs are so big.

As healing continues to take root in souls and mind of the Beneficiary of Mt Elgon, they realise just how deep and complex trauma is in shaping a community and human.

A lot of voices have spoken and testify how the program has moved them and they have a big shift in their social lifestyle and their livelihood.

Still more young people who have not undergone the trainings are still in need of these trainings. Our work revolves around healing and creating a positive Respond to the social challenges we are facing.

Now we are faced by the need of a complex community that is rooted in deep cultural patterns that again hurts children and women without pay attention to most of the things surrounding peaceful healing. Some of the key things that we realized are listed below;

a.       Women empowerment, in all the dimension women are subject and not equals to me.

This being a big challenge in the whole world, issues like using family planning are not talked about and thus one can have as many children as they believe God gives them. So many parents are very young and get themselves having children that it becomes very difficult to support their medical, educational and basic needs.

One of the basic program is empowering women and help each other to look at how to bring in life a health family and learning to talk about feelings with their men.

b.      Both the girls and young children are facing so many risks in life.

Girls are sexually abused by either relative, teachers or motorbike transporters just in the name that they cannot

afford Sanitary Towels thus they are at the mercies of this individual at a certain time of their life. In just 7 months defilement of minors’ case became so rampant and it brought a lot of concerns.

c.       The sad thing is when a parent who is supposed to protect the child especially the fathers go ahead and negotiate payments just to get rid of the Law despite whatever laws are in the country.

d.      In this the woman has no say and thus the victims usually end up not having justice. As we talked to the police hoping to get help in stopping this, we only realized even women when they go to fetch firewood from the forest, they are raped but can’t say to the administration because they feel it is a shame.

e.       The community still deeply use the skills of tradition midwives, who again are not having any experience.

A lot of mortality death is experienced but not reported since they try to help but have a difficult time if it is a complicated birth.

Again, it is a culture challenge where most women will not go to hospital because they don’t want the male doctors to attend to them, issues related to HIV is still needs a lot of awareness.

There is little knowledge about STI and HIV.

f.       There are lots of Orphans that have been neglected in the care of elderly grandparents or abusive relative,

they manage to go to school on a good day but without school uniforms they feel odd and unsettled to be in class, so some opt go for manual jobs and

save money to buy books and school uniforms. Girls stay out of school during their menstrual circles and even as they go to school they have never

experience people who visit and give motivational talk. In some situations, teachers get overwhelmed because of the number of students they serve and lessons per day that they lose to concentrate on follow up.

g.       The Organization was founded with the community need of having a peace center, to play as a neutral place where the communities

from both clans can meet and share skills and have dialogues about the progress of their communities.

We have a two and half acre of land and In it there is a small building that can’t fit the number of participants in some of the meetings at the same time have facilitators stay in especially when it rains.

This center is constructed it will give some of the orphan who are having extreme harsh condition a home to feel love and acceptance and look at life with hope,

it will also provide a library for school kids and high school students to have time of reading and opening their mind wider with that opportunity.

Plan is also to have a workshop conference with rooms that communities can use for peaceful and economic trainings as they happen.
